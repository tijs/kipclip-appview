## Design Context

### Users

Bluesky / AT Protocol users who want a simple, reliable bookmark manager. They
value data ownership — their bookmarks live on their own PDS, not kipclip's
servers. Context of use: saving links throughout the day from desktop and
mobile, then returning to find or read them later. Not power users managing
thousands of links — people who want a trustworthy place to put things they want
to find again.

### Brand Personality

**Friendly, simple, trustworthy.** kipclip should feel like a well-made everyday
tool — reliable, warm, and easy to pick up. Kip the chicken adds character and
approachability but shouldn't make the app feel like a toy. Think of a favorite
notebook: it has personality in its cover, but the pages inside are clean and
functional.

Three words: **friendly, simple, trustworthy.**

### Aesthetic Direction

**North star: Things 3 / Bear.** Apple-quality polish with subtle details and
beautiful typography. Feels premium but never complex. The design should
disappear into the content — bookmarks, tags, and reading lists are the star.

**Existing palette (warm and intentional):**

- Coral `#e66456` — primary actions
- Cream `#f5f1e8` — background warmth
- Teal `#5b8a8f` — secondary accent
- Orange `#f4a261` — available for highlights

**Theme: Light.** The warm cream background is a signature. Keep it.

**Anti-references (all selected — strong boundaries):**

- Not a generic SaaS dashboard (no corporate blues, no metric cards)
- Not a social media feed (no engagement patterns, no noise)
- Not a developer tool (no dark mode defaults, no monospace aesthetic)
- Not overly cute or childish (Kip adds warmth, not whimsy)

### Design Principles

1. **Content is king.** Bookmarks, titles, and tags should be the most prominent
   things on screen. UI chrome should recede.
2. **Quiet confidence.** Polish shows in spacing, typography, and transitions —
   not in decorative effects. Like Things 3: you feel the quality without
   pointing to any single flashy element.
3. **Warm, not cute.** The coral-and-cream palette and Kip give kipclip soul.
   Lean into warmth without crossing into playful or childish.
4. **Respect the user's time.** Fast interactions, clear hierarchy, no
   unnecessary steps. Every element earns its place.
5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG AA compliance. Good contrast, keyboard
   navigation, screen reader support. Accessibility is not an afterthought.

### Accessibility

- WCAG AA compliance
- Sufficient contrast ratios on the warm palette (cream backgrounds need careful
  text contrast)
- Keyboard navigable throughout
- Screen reader friendly with proper ARIA labels
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px on mobile
